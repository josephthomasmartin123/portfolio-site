KnotPulse

A concept for targeted resonant electrical muscle therapy — using frequency sweeps to selectively activate and release deep muscle knots.

Date Mar 2026 Type Concept Status Pre-prototype

Motivation

Picture me. A chronic goer of gym for many years, plagued with the disease of 'CBA to stretch'. This culminated in many years of muscle knots that I thought were a fundamental part of my physiology.

I was also lucky enough to go on holiday to Thailand, where I got my first Thai massage. It was here that some poor Thai lady was tasked with removing these knots that I didn't even know I had, some of the biggest she had ever seen apparently! This experience was magical and it's something I would love to have on demand, but massage guns don't really do it for me and prices are COOKED in the UK. So whilst on the table in a dreamy state I got to thinking about how I can make a device to melt away muscle knots. The concept written up here is largely the same as dreamt up on the table — it was quite nice to brainstorm without any stimulus or internet to help out for once.

Abstract

A muscle knot releasing device that uses the muscles themselves to generate the mechanical deformation required to break up muscle knots by pulsing them with electricity at the mechanical resonant frequency of the muscle itself.

Introduction to Muscle Knots (crash course)

It's fairly well understood that:

  • Muscles contract when stimulated by nerve impulses, which takes the form of an electrical signal through the nervous system
  • When these muscles contract, there is a biomechanical reaction where ATP of cells (consider it the currency of muscle energy) is utilised to create work.
  • Muscle knots are localised regions of muscle tissue that can't relax — they're actually void of ATP but can't actually cross that energy threshold to relax. You can think analogously of it as a company going under with debt.
  • Within that muscle knot is actually a really poor biochemical environment, primarily characterised as a low pH (lots of lactic acid), that can't be flushed with the surrounding blood, hence the cramping.
  • When massaging, fluid is pushed in and out of the knot which is one of the fundamental mechanisms that releases it

That's a massive oversimplification, but it was my understanding of muscle knots on the table, and I validated it at a high level upon writing this up.

Concepting

The goal is to move fluid through muscle knots using mechanical deformation. Inspired by a deep thigh massage, I wondered if we could massage tissue from within since external pressure often fails to reach deep fibers. By using electrical stimulation to trigger the muscle's own elastic resonance, we could create significant internal deformation and fluid flow. The challenge lies in identifying and driving the muscle at its specific resonant frequency, which likely varies based on muscle type, hydration, and biochemical state. Therefore can we run live frequency sweeps with sensors to identify the resonant frequency of the muscle in that particular condition?

Concept

A muscle knot releasing device that uses the muscles themselves to generate the mechanical deformation required to break up muscle knots by pulsing them with electricity at the mechanical resonant frequency of the muscle itself. The device sweeps through a range of activation frequencies to identify resonance, then resonates the muscle. The system may alternate with the resonant frequency of the lymphatic system to encourage lactic drainage.

The device is based on a TENS machine with built in sensors, such as an accelerometer, that can measure the spatial displacement and acceleration of the muscle.
