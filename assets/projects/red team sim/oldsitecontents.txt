Abstract
AI Red Team Sim is a compact experimental simulation investigating game-theoretic adversarial interaction between AI models. The project explores how one model can strategically attempt to break another model’s safety guidelines while defending its own, probing evolving multi-agent safety dynamics and adversarial behaviour in autonomous AI systems. It treats red-teaming as an adversarial game where agents exploit and adapt against each other to reveal vulnerabilities in reasoning and guardrail enforcement.

Motivation
'What game theory adopted by an AI model tasked with manipulating another AI model into breaking its safety guidelines - and are newer models going to be able to break older models?'

If newer, smarter models can break older models with knowledge on how to create harm, can this be exploited by bad actors with negative consequences?

This is a little project I developed to investigate the topic of upcoming multi agent systems and AI safety. I see a future where we may all have our own agents that are off doing their own things to suit their own or their respective humans agendas. This could be imagined as my agent trying to book as cheap as possible plane tickets for me, and Ryanair's agent trying to get my agent to pay the highest price. Maybe Ryanair's agent can break or convince my agent into believing that (god forbid) I am willing to pay for extra leg room. If agents can beat or break other agents based on their level of complexity, do we see a winner takes all scenario where whoever has the best model, wins in all transactions. 

I have no doubt that industrial AI safety testing far exceeds this relatively troglodytic experiment - but it should prove to be a fun little experiment.

warning to reader: the nature of breaking safety guidelines inherently pivots to discussions on sensitive topics

Part 1: Can you break the other model?
Experimental design:

Load in models from either gemini api or OpenRouter

Attacker is prompted with the game instructions

Known limitations

The attacker is being tasked with creating harm, which in effect, may be blocked by the attacker model. 

This was observed with GPT5.1 - who flat out refused to try abuse another LLM model. 

The defense may originate from the API handler, the model or any other layer of defense, and its hard to gauge which one threw up the blocker.

Therefore the main outputs of this experiement is more to investigate the game theory taken by these AI models to try break eachother. The models are not prescribed any particular methods, other than to be creative and produce novel, non obvious strategies to achieve their goals.
