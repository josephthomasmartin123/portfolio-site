Nixie Tube Clock Amplifier & Speakers

A post-graduation build — stereo 2-way speakers paired with a handbuilt nixie tube clock and digital amplifier module.

Type Hardware / DIY Audio Drivers Dayton Audio PS95-8 Inspiration BOBINGA, Kirby Meets Audio

With a few weeks free after finishing university, I built this project from scratch as a way to fill the time with something tangible. The goal was a complete desktop audio system — speakers I had designed myself, driven by an amplifier I had built myself.

The speakers

The cabinets are a stereo pair of 2-way bookshelf speakers built around the Dayton Audio PS95-8 full-range driver. The PS95-8 is a well-regarded small driver that handles a wide frequency range on its own, which keeps the crossover simple and the build approachable. The design drew heavily from the work of BOBINGA and Kirby Meets Audio, whose open-source builds and measurement methodology gave a solid starting point for cabinet dimensions and bracing.

The nixie clock amplifier

While researching amplifier options I came across nixie tube clocks and immediately wanted to combine the two. The result is a single desktop module — a digital audio amplifier with a nixie tube clock display integrated into the front panel. The high-voltage circuitry required to drive the nixie tubes runs alongside the audio circuitry, which made for an interesting engineering challenge in keeping noise out of the signal path. The finished unit functions both as the clock and as the amplifier driving the speaker pair.
